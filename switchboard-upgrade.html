How to Know If You Need a Switchboard Upgrade

You Have a Fuse Box

If your home still has ceramic fuses or a porcelain fuse box, it's time for an upgrade. These outdated systems don't provide the safety features of modern circuit breakers.

Frequent Tripping

If your circuit breakers or fuses frequently trip or blow, it could indicate that your switchboard can't handle your home's electrical load.

Flickering Lights

Lights that dim or flicker when you use appliances may indicate an overloaded circuit or inadequate power supply from your switchboard.

Older Home

If your home was built before 1990, it likely has an outdated switchboard that wasn't designed for today's electrical demands.

No Safety Switches

Modern homes require safety switches (RCDs) on all circuits. If your switchboard doesn't have these, it needs upgrading to comply with current regulations.

Renovation Plans

If you're planning to renovate or add new appliances, your current switchboard may not have the capacity to handle the increased load.

Benefits of Upgrading Your Switchboard

Enhanced Safety

Modern circuit breakers respond faster to electrical faults than old fuses, significantly reducing the risk of electrical fires and electrocution. Safety switches (RCDs) can detect even minor current leakages and shut off power within milliseconds.

Increased Capacity

Modern switchboards can handle more circuits and higher power demands, allowing you to safely use multiple appliances simultaneously and add new electrical features to your home without overloading the system.

Reduced Energy Bills

A new switchboard can improve energy efficiency by distributing power more effectively throughout your home, potentially leading to lower electricity bills.

Compliance with Standards

Upgrading ensures your electrical system meets current Australian Standards and safety regulations, which is essential for insurance purposes and when selling your property.

Increased Property Value

A modern, compliant switchboard is an attractive feature for potential buyers and can increase your property's market value and appeal.

Future-Proofing

A new switchboard provides capacity for future electrical additions, such as air conditioning, EV charging, solar panels, or home automation systems.

Switchboard Upgrade Costs

The cost of a switchboard upgrade depends on several factors. While we provide personalized quotes after assessment, here are the main factors that influence pricing:

Property Size

Larger homes typically require more circuits and a larger switchboard, increasing the overall cost.

Electrical Requirements

The number of circuits needed, power capacity requirements, and special needs (like three-phase power) affect the final price.

Complexity of Installation

Difficult access, extensive rewiring needs, or relocating the switchboard position can increase labor costs.

Safety Equipment

The number of safety switches (RCDs) and surge protectors required will impact the total cost.

Switchboard Quality

Premium switchboards with advanced features cost more but offer better protection and longer lifespans.

Permits and Compliance

Costs for electrical permits, inspections, and certificates of compliance are factored into the final price.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a switchboard upgrade take?

A typical switchboard upgrade takes between 4-8 hours to complete, depending on the complexity of the installation. We'll provide a more specific timeframe after assessing your current setup.

Will I need to be without power during the entire upgrade?

Yes, we'll need to disconnect power to your property while replacing the switchboard. We schedule this at a convenient time and work efficiently to minimize downtime.

Are safety switches mandatory in Victoria?

Yes, Victorian regulations require safety switches (RCDs) on all power circuits. When upgrading your switchboard, we ensure compliance with all current safety standards.

Will I need council approval for a switchboard upgrade?

Generally, you don't need council approval for a like-for-like switchboard replacement. However, significant electrical system changes might require permits. We handle all necessary paperwork.

How often should a switchboard be replaced?

Switchboards typically last 25-40 years, but it depends on the quality of components and changing electrical needs. We recommend an inspection if your switchboard is older than 20 years.
